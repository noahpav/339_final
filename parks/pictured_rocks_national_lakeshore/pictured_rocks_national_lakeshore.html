Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore

Pictured Rocks National Lakeshore, located along the southern shore of Lake Superior in Michigan’s Upper Peninsula, is a breathtaking destination known for its colorful sandstone cliffs, pristine beaches, and dramatic waterfalls. Spanning over 40 miles of shoreline, the park offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and outdoor adventure. Visitors can explore crystal-clear waters, hike through lush forests, and marvel at the striking rock formations that give the park its name.

What They Offer

  • Hiking: Discover over 100 miles of trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes. Popular routes include the Chapel Loop Trail and Miners Castle Trail.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Paddle along the base of the cliffs for an up-close view of the colorful rock formations, sea caves, and waterfalls.
  • Boat Tours: Take a guided boat tour to enjoy stunning views of the cliffs, beaches, and surrounding forests from the water.
  • Camping: Experience the great outdoors at one of the park’s backcountry or drive-in campgrounds. Wake up to the sound of waves and birdsong.
  • Photography: Capture iconic landmarks like Miners Castle, Chapel Rock, and Bridalveil Falls. The park’s sunsets over Lake Superior are particularly spectacular.
  • Winter Activities: Enjoy snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, or ice climbing during the colder months.
  • Wildlife Watching: Spot wildlife like black bears, white-tailed deer, and a variety of birds in their natural habitats.

Some History

Pictured Rocks has long been a place of cultural and natural significance. The cliffs were named for their vivid colors and unique patterns, caused by mineral stains of iron, manganese, and copper. For centuries, the area was home to Native American tribes, particularly the Ojibwe, who lived off the land and used the waterways for travel and trade.

In the 19th century, the region became an important site for logging and shipping, as its location on Lake Superior made it a hub for transporting lumber and other goods. Recognizing its unique beauty and ecological importance, Pictured Rocks was designated as the United States’ first national lakeshore in 1966. Today, it is celebrated for its rich history, scenic landscapes, and outdoor recreation opportunities.
