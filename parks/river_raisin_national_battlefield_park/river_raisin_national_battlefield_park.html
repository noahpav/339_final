River Raisin National Battlefield Park

River Raisin National Battlefield Park, located in Monroe, Michigan, commemorates the pivotal events of the War of 1812. The site preserves the memory of the Battle of the River Raisin, a tragic yet significant moment in American history. Known for the rallying cry “Remember the Raisin,” the park honors the sacrifices of those who fought while educating visitors about the war’s impact on the nation. With interactive exhibits, historic grounds, and engaging programs, the park is a meaningful destination for history enthusiasts and families alike.

What They Offer

  • Interactive Exhibits: Visit the visitor center to explore exhibits that detail the events of the battle, the War of 1812, and the broader historical context.
  • Historic Walking Trails: Stroll along marked trails through the battlefield, featuring interpretive signs that guide visitors through key moments of the conflict.
  • Ranger-Led Programs: Attend ranger-led talks and reenactments that bring the history of the battlefield to life.
  • Living History Events: Experience living history demonstrations, including period costumes, weapon displays, and battle reenactments.
  • Junior Ranger Program: Engage children in learning about history through interactive activities and earning a Junior Ranger badge.
  • Special Events: Participate in annual commemorative events, including the Battle of the River Raisin anniversary programs in January.

Some History

The Battle of the River Raisin, fought in January 1813, was one of the largest engagements of the War of 1812. American forces clashed with British troops and their Native American allies in a fight for control of southeastern Michigan. After initial success, American forces suffered a devastating defeat on January 22, 1813, with many killed, wounded, or taken captive. The aftermath saw atrocities committed against wounded soldiers, fueling the rallying cry, “Remember the Raisin,” which motivated American forces in subsequent battles.
